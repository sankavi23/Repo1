1.vehicle - a conveyance that transports people or objects
2.passenger - a traveler riding in a vehicle but not operating it
3.transportation - the movement of humans, animals and goods from one location to another. 
4.airport - an airfield equipped with control tower and hangars as well as accommodations for passengers and cargo
5.drive - operate or control a vehicle

6.traffic - vehicles or pedestrians traveling in a particular locality

7.bus - a vehicle carrying many passengers

8.car - a motor vehicle with four wheels
9.pedestrian - a person who travels by foot

10.ride - sit and travel on the back of animal, usually while controlling its motions
11.journey - the act of traveling from one place to another
12.bicycle - a wheeled vehicle that has two wheels and is moved by foot pedals
13.lorry -  a large truck designed to carry heavy loads; usually without sides

14.road -  hard surface built for vehicles to travel along
15.ship - a vessel that carries passengers or freight
16.aeroplane - a fixed-wing aircraft that is powered by propellers or jets

17.train - a set of several carriages that are connected to each other and pulled along a railway line by an engine
18.pilot - a person who operates the flying controls of an aircraft.
19.captain - the person in command of a ship
20.crowded - overfilled or compacted or concentrated
21.departure - the act of leaving
22.arrive - reach a destination
23.ticket - a commercial document showing that the holder is entitled to something
24.luggage - cases used to carry belongings when traveling
25.station - a regular stopping place (as on a bus, train, or subway line)
